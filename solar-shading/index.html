External Solar Shading

The only shading that
satisfies Part O.

Internal curtains, blinds and shutters cannot meet the UK's overheating regulations. External solar shading can. We design, supply and install roller screens, louvre systems, awnings and motorised pergolas across Sussex, Kent, Surrey and London — and beyond.

Request a Specification Part O Guide
Why External?

Regulation changed the game.

Part O of the Building Regulations (in force 15 June 2022) mandates overheating mitigation in new residential buildings. The Technical Guidance is unambiguous: internal blinds, curtains and shutters — however premium — do not count. Only external solar shading satisfies the standard.

The Future Homes Standard (laid March 2026, in force March 2027) extends and tightens this requirement. If you are an architect, developer or facilities manager, external shading is now a compliance necessity, not a specification luxury.

Applications

Who installs external shading.

Residential

Glazed extensions, orangeries and south-facing bedrooms. We advise on the right system for each elevation and install with minimal disruption.

Independent Schools

Overheated classrooms reduce concentration and exam performance. External shading is the correct, long-term solution. We install in holiday periods to avoid disruption.

Hotels & Hospitality

Guest comfort in summer months, terrace extensions with louvre pergolas, restaurant and bar outdoor areas. Contract-grade product, trade terms available.

Healthcare

HTM-compliant external shading for wards, treatment rooms and corridors. We work with NHS estates teams and private healthcare operators.

Commercial Offices

BREEAM-creditable external shading for EPC improvement and occupant comfort. Louvre systems and external Venetians for glazed facades.

Developers

Part O compliance built into new-build specification from the outset. We work with developers from planning stage through to practical completion.
