Why Shutters?

Light control without
fabric maintenance.

Shutters are permanently fitted to the window frame — there is no fabric to fade, clean or replace. The louvre blades can be adjusted to any angle throughout the day. Fully open, they fold back flat against the frame and disappear entirely.

They work particularly well in sash-window townhouses, bay-window Victorian and Edwardian houses, and any room where privacy is important and views need to be maintained. In wet areas — kitchens and bathrooms — moisture-resistant MDF outperforms both real timber and fabric alternatives.

Shutter Configurations

The right style for
your windows.

Full Height

A single panel running the full height of the window opening. The most popular configuration. Simple, clean, and maximally effective for light and privacy control. Works on most window types.

Cafe Style

Shutters on the lower half of the window only. Gives privacy at street level while maintaining an open, light-filled upper window. Common in Victorian terraces and period cottages with sash windows.

Tier-on-Tier

Upper and lower panels that operate independently. Upper panels can be open while lower panels are closed for privacy, or vice versa. Excellent light flexibility. Suited to tall sash windows.

Bay Windows

Tracked shutter systems that run around the full perimeter of a bay. The panels slide on a track rather than folding, avoiding the problem of limited fold-back space in a deep bay recess.

Solid Panel Shutters

No louvre blades — a solid painted panel. Used for blackout in bedrooms, for rooms where no outward view is required, or purely as a decorative architectural element. Very clean, minimal appearance.

Moisture-Resistant (Kitchen & Bathroom)

MDF-core shutters with moisture-resistant paint finish. Will not warp or delaminate in humid environments. Indistinguishable from real timber shutters in appearance. Easier to maintain in kitchens and bathrooms.

Materials

Solid timber or
moisture-resistant MDF.

We offer both solid timber and MDF shutter panels depending on the application. For dry living spaces — drawing rooms, bedrooms, hallways — solid timber is the premium choice. For kitchens, bathrooms and wet rooms, moisture-resistant MDF is the more practical and equally good-looking option.

All shutters are factory-primed and then painted in the colour of your choice — RAL or Farrow & Ball. We can match any existing interior woodwork colour.

Louvre Sizes

Blade size affects
the look.

Larger louvre blades give a more open, contemporary appearance and better views when open. Smaller blades give a more traditional look and better privacy when angled. We discuss blade size as part of the initial consultation.
