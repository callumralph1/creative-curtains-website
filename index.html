The Regulatory Shift

Part O is law.
Internal blinds don't count.

Since 15 June 2022, Part O of the Building Regulations has made overheating mitigation mandatory in new residential builds. Internal curtains, blinds and shutters do not satisfy the requirement. Only external solar shading does.

The Future Homes Standard, laid in March 2026 and in force from March 2027, tightens this further. If you are specifying, developing or retrofitting, external shading is no longer optional.

How We Work

From first contact
to finished room.

  1. 01

    Consultation

    We visit your property or meet at our Hailsham showroom. We talk through the brief — light control, thermal performance, aesthetics, regulation — and recommend accordingly. No hard sell.

  2. 02

    Design & Specification

    We produce a written specification with fabric samples, product datasheets and, where relevant, TM59 or BREEAM performance data. Architects and designers receive drawings in the format they need.

  3. 03

    Manufacture & Supply

    Everything is made to measure. Curtains and blinds made in the UK. External shading systems from European manufacturers with full CE and UKCA certification where required.

    Installation & Handover

    Our own fitters, not subcontractors. We dress the room, test every motorised component and hand over a written care guide. Snagging is handled by the same person who did the install.
