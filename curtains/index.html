The Difference

Measured for your room,
not adapted to it.

Off-the-shelf curtains are made to standard drops and widths, then hemmed or pinned to fit. Made-to-measure curtains are cut from scratch to the exact dimensions of your windows — correct stack-back, correct drop, correct fullness for the heading style.

We visit your property, take precise measurements, and guide you through fabric choice and heading options. There is no ambiguity about what you will receive.

Heading Styles

Choose the right heading
for your room.

Wave Heading

Clean, contemporary folds of even depth. Requires a specialist track. The preferred heading for modern interiors, hotel bedrooms and any scheme where a tailored, uncluttered look is required.

Pencil Pleat

Dense, tightly gathered pleats. The classic British heading. Works on both poles and tracks. Generous fabric requirement — typically 2.5x the track width — gives a full, traditional look.

Eyelet

Metal eyelets threaded directly onto a pole. Gives a relaxed, informal fold. Easy to draw and re-draw. Better suited to lighter-weight fabrics — heavy linings can make drawing difficult.

Pinch Pleat

Double or triple pinch pleats, hand-sewn. A dressmaker heading that gives structured, elegant folds. Works beautifully in high-ceilinged rooms and formal settings.

Goblet Pleat

A goblet-shaped pleat at the header. More formal than pinch pleat, very well suited to heavy fabrics and formal rooms. Often padded inside for shape retention.

Tab Top

Fabric loops sewn at the header, threaded onto a decorative pole. Informal and relaxed in character. Best for lightweight rooms and window dressings rather than heavy blackout applications.

Lining Options

The right lining
makes a room.

Lining is not a detail — it affects how the curtain hangs, how much light it blocks, how well it insulates and how long it lasts. We discuss lining as part of the initial consultation.

Standard Cotton Lining

Improves hang, provides modest insulation and light reduction. The minimum for any quality curtain.

Interlining

A thick wadding layer between face fabric and lining. Significantly improves drape, insulation and sound absorption. The difference is visible and tactile.

Blackout Lining

Full blackout for bedrooms, media rooms and hotel bedrooms. Available with or without interlining. Eliminates light leakage at edges when combined with a good track or pole system.
