Blind Types

Choose the right blind
for each room.

Roman Blinds

Fabric folds flat when raised, creating clean horizontal stacks. The most versatile blind style — works in period and contemporary rooms alike. Available lined, interlined and blackout. Made in any fabric.

Roller Blinds

Clean, minimal and practical. Available in blackout, solar-control (dimout) and translucent fabrics. Chain-operated or motorised. Frequently specified for bedrooms, kitchens and commercial applications.

Venetian Blinds

Aluminium, timber and faux-wood slat options. Fine light control — the slat angle can be adjusted to direct light up toward the ceiling or down toward the floor. 25mm and 50mm slat widths.

Cellular (Honeycomb) Blinds

Excellent thermal insulation properties due to the air pockets in the honeycomb cell structure. Available in single and double-cell configurations. Top-down / bottom-up options. Good for difficult windows.

Vertical Louvre Blinds

Fabric or PVC louvres that rotate 180 degrees for full light control. Practical for wide windows and patio doors. Contract-grade PVC louvres are common in education and healthcare. Wipe-clean and highly durable.

Recommended

Motorised Blinds

Any blind type can be motorised. We specify Somfy motors as standard — reliable, whisper-quiet and integrable with smart home platforms including Apple HomeKit, Google Home and Crestron. Battery or hardwired.

Important Note

Internal blinds and
Part O compliance.

If you are looking for solar control for a new-build or extensively refurbished property, it is important to know that internal blinds — including solar-control roller blinds and Venetian blinds — do not satisfy Part O of the Building Regulations.

Only external solar shading counts toward Part O overheating compliance. We offer both — and we will tell you honestly which you need.

Explore External Solar Shading

Internal blinds are ideal for:

  • ✓Blackout in bedrooms where external shading is not practical
  • ✓Light diffusion in south-facing offices and living rooms
  • ✓Commercial and healthcare environments where wipe-clean is required
  • ✓Existing buildings where external installation is not permitted or practical
